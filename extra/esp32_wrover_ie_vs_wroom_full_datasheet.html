ESP32-WROVER-IE N16R8
Datasheet v2.3 · Active product
Pros (from datasheet)
8 MB PSRAM — heap extends to ~4 MB with Wi-Fi active
16 MB flash — large OTA + LittleFS + firmware all fit
ESP32-D0WD-V3 chip rev3 only — no ECO patches needed
IPEX/u.FL connector — Hirose U.FL / I-PEX MHF I compatible
External antenna: max gain 2.33 dBi (certified monopole)
Camera applications: PSRAM holds full DVP frame buffer
Active product — not NRND
Cons (from datasheet)
IO16 & IO17 reserved — connected to PSRAM SPI, do not use
31.4 mm long — 5.9 mm longer than WROOM, not drop-in
Requires external antenna + IPEX cable (not included)
PSRAM adds board-level current spikes during burst access
Higher BOM cost and assembly complexity
Best for (from applications list)
Camera streaming, speech recognition, image recognition, audio devices, large IoT data loggers, TLS/HTTPS with large payloads
ESP32-WROOM-32D / 32U
Datasheet v2.4 · NRND
Pros (from datasheet)
IO16 & IO17 free — UART2 fully available
32D: 25.5 mm — smaller, easier to fit on tight boards
32D: built-in PCB trace antenna — zero extra components
32U: same tiny footprint + IPEX connector for external antenna
Lower current spikes (no PSRAM activity)
Lowest cost, widest availability, massive community
8/16 MB flash variants available on custom order
Cons (from datasheet)
NRND — Espressif does not recommend for new designs
No PSRAM — heap ~160–200 KB with Wi-Fi on
4 MB flash default — OTA + LittleFS requires careful partitioning
ESP32-D0WD chip may be rev1 — check errata / ECO document
Camera/audio: no frame buffer RAM available
HTTPS/TLS: large cert chains risk heap exhaustion
Best for
Simple sensors, MQTT, UART2 required, cost-sensitive, open-air installs (32D), existing designs only (NRND)
